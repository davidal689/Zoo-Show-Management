Introduction
Purpose
This Software Requirements Specification (SRS) describes the functional and nonfunctional requirements for Release 1.0 of the Show Management System (SMS), a web-based platform that lets water park visitors browse show schedules and purchase tickets online, and gives park staff centralized tools to manage shows, issue tickets, and oversee operations. This document is intended for the members of the project team who will design, implement, and verify the correct functioning of the system. Unless otherwise noted, all requirements specified here are committed for Release 1.0.
Document Conventions
Functional requirements are organized by use case within user class, following the convention ID FR-UC-N, where UC is the two-digit use case number from the Use Case Specification [2] and N is a sequential number within that use case (e.g., FR-04-3 is the third functional requirement derived from UC-04). Each use-case group states a single priority level (High, Medium, or Low), carried over directly from that use case's Priority field. Nonfunctional and supporting requirements use the category prefixes defined in the relevant section (e.g., OE- for Operating Environment, SEC- for Security). Where a requirement enforces or derives from a specific business rule, the rule is cited in parentheses using its BR-NN identifier from the Business Rules Document [3]; these citations are traceability references, not restatements, and the authoritative rule text remains in [3]. As in the referenced templates, all statements of system behavior use ‘shall’.

Overall Description
Product Perspectives
The Show Management System is a new, standalone web application that replaces the water park's current manual and counter-based processes for publishing show schedules and selling tickets. Show schedules are presently available only at park entrances or static notice boards, and ticketing is handled manually at physical counters, which is prone to overbooking and gives management little real-time visibility into attendance. The SMS centralizes show information and ticket purchasing through a browser-accessible platform used directly by visitors, and through role-specific interfaces used by cashiers, show managers, and administrators.

User Classes and Characteristics
Park Visitor | A Park Visitor is a public water park patron who accesses the Show Management System online to discover live entertainment and secure show admissions. Park Visitors interact with the system via standard desktop, laptop, or personal mobile web browsers using their personal devices or shared terminals. Their core responsibilities within the application include browsing published show schedules, viewing real-time seat availability, registering and authenticating their accounts, processing online ticket purchases via the single integrated payment gateway, and viewing or cancelling their own active bookings. The frequency of use for this class is High, generating the largest aggregate transaction volume on the platform, with peak activity occurring during morning and early afternoon hours. Water park management aims to have at least 50 percent of all ticket sales completed online by this user class within 6 months of release, with a stretch goal of 70 percent. While visitors are generally enthusiastic about the platform, they may initially be unfamiliar with online ticketing workflows, necessitating an intuitive user interface that offers immediate, clear on-screen confirmation messages. This user class is strictly constrained by role-based access control, preventing them from viewing any other user’s data or accessing cashier, show management, or administrative functions.

Cashier | A Cashier is a front-line water park employee who utilizes the point-of-sale interface at physical park ticketing counters to process walk-in visitors. Their core responsibilities include searching or selecting shows, checking real-time seating availability, inputting requested ticket quantities, processing transactions through the integrated payment gateway, and looking up existing orders by name or booking reference number. The frequency of use for this class is High, operating continuously as the primary transactional channel for walk-in traffic during peak park operating hours. Cashiers are bound by a strict operational efficiency constraint requiring them to complete a counter ticket transaction in an average of 1.5 minutes or less to minimize line wait times. Cashiers are cautiously receptive to the software and highly concerned about system performance and UI simplicity under time pressure, requiring basic operational orientation before the system goes live. Role-based boundaries completely block cashiers from accessing visitor account management or administrative reporting functions.

Show Manager | A Show Manager is an operational park staff member responsible for organizing and publishing live performance events. Their core responsibilities involve navigating the show management panel to create new show entries, input show details (such as names, descriptions, venues, times, and initial capacities), save listings as invisible drafts, publish active shows to the public listings, or cancel scheduled events. The frequency of use for this class is Moderate, with usage peaking before the launch of new park seasons or when operational changes necessitate scheduling updates. System efficiency goals dictate that a Show Manager must be able to create, update, and instantly publish a show listing entry in less than 10 minutes using the administrative interface. Show Managers are highly receptive to digital coordination but possess limited overall technical proficiency, meaning they require a straightforward, form-driven, task-oriented user interface that minimizes complex navigation. They will receive a basic system orientation prior to deployment. System validations strictly prohibit a Show Manager from reducing a show's seat capacity below the total number of tickets already sold for that show, and prevent them from editing or cancelling any show that has already taken place.

Administrator | An Administrator is a dedicated operational or IT staff member responsible for systemic oversight of the platform post-launch. Their core responsibilities include accessing the administrative dashboard to manage user accounts (including creating, editing, and deactivating staff profiles), enforce strict single-role assignments, and generate automated transaction and attendance reports. The frequency of use for this class is Moderate, performing user modifications infrequently while generating operational reports on a weekly or on-demand basis. Administrators are bound by a strict nonfunctional requirement ensuring that comprehensive report generation across any specified custom date range completes entirely within 2 minutes. Administrators are fully supportive of the system and are expected to possess the highest technical literacy among all human user classes. The water park must designate at least one administrator to oversee the platform after deployment. Role permissions strictly bar administrators from cancelling or deleting any scheduled shows, a responsibility reserved exclusively for Show Managers.

Data Requirements
Logical Data Model
The SMS is organized around six core entities. A Visitor Account or Staff Account (role: Show Manager, Cashier, or Administrator) identifies a user and their single assigned role (BR-01). A Show is created and maintained by a Show Manager and has a status of Draft, Published, or Cancelled, together with a total and remaining seat capacity. A Booking links one Show to either a Visitor Account (online channel) or a Cashier-issued counter sale, and records the ticket quantity, total price, unique reference number, and status (Confirmed or Cancelled). Each finalized Booking is associated with one Payment record, which stores the outcome (Success or Failed) of the corresponding payment gateway transaction; the gateway itself, not the SMS, retains the underlying payment card data (BR-22). Every administrative action and denied access attempt is recorded as an Audit Log Entry, referencing the acting account. Relationships: one Show has many Bookings; one Visitor Account has many Bookings; one Staff Account (Show Manager) manages many Shows; one Booking has at most one Payment; many Audit Log Entries reference one account.
